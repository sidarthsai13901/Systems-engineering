There are eight technical management processes used in systems engineering. 
These processes support the design, development, operation, and management of a system throughout its entire life cycle, from concept to disposal.
# 6.1 Technical Planning 
## Technical Planning Process
The **Technical Planning Process** establishes how systems engineering activities will be planned, managed, and executed throughout the system lifecycle. It ensures the project achieves its technical objectives while meeting **cost, schedule, and risk** constraints.
### Key Objectives
- Develop a structured plan for all systems engineering activities.
- Define the technical effort required to meet project objectives and lifecycle success criteria.
- Establish clear team roles, responsibilities, and project goals.
- Create and baseline the **Systems Engineering Management Plan (SEMP)** and supporting technical plans.
- Align technical plans with the overall project master schedule and management plans.
- Plan technical contracting activities and resource allocation.
- Continuously monitor and update plans as the project evolves.
### Best Practices
- Start technical planning as early as possible.
- Keep technical plans synchronized with project management plans.
- Review and update plans at every **Key Decision Point (KDP)** or whenever major project changes occur.
- Assess the impact of requirement, resource, budget, or stakeholder changes before updating the project baseline.
### Deliverables
- Systems Engineering Management Plan (SEMP)
- Technical Management Plans
- Technical Schedule
- Resource & Budget Plan
- Risk-Based Technical Execution Plan
## Crosscutting Technical Management Keys
- **Plan Early** – Define the technical scope, Product Breakdown Structure (PBS), schedule, workflow, resources, budget, facilities, and constraints.
- **Define Interfaces** – Clearly identify subsystem and organizational interfaces, assign ownership, and manage compatibility.
- **Configuration Management** – Track all design changes to maintain system integrity and ensure analyses remain valid.
- **Milestone Reviews** – Conduct technical reviews only when predefined entry criteria are met to assess project readiness.
- **Document Assumptions** – Record all assumptions, constraints, and potential biases that influence engineering decisions.
- **Manage Technical Data** – Keep engineering documents, models, and analyses under configuration control for traceability.
- **Continuous Updates** – Regularly update technical plans, schedules, and analyses as requirements, risks, or resources change.
- **Traceability** – Ensure every requirement, design decision, test, and verification activity is fully traceable throughout the system lifecycle.

# Inputs

## 1. Project Technical Effort Requirements & Project Resource Constraints
- Project technical requirements
- Budget allocation
- Schedule constraints
- Resource availability
- Develop technical cost estimates and schedules based on actual work.
- Continuously reconcile technical estimates with allocated budget and schedule throughout the project lifecycle.

## 2. Agreements, Capability Needs & Applicable Product Life Cycle Phase
- Applicable life cycle phases
- Project milestones
- Internal and external agreements
- Capability needs
- Establish the framework for planning technical activities and deliverables.

## 3. Applicable Policies, Procedures, Standards & Organizational Processes
- Organizational policies
- Engineering procedures
- Applicable standards
- Organizational development processes
- Ensure the technical approach complies with all mandated project requirements.

## 4. Prior Phase or Baseline Plans
- Previous life cycle plans
- Baselined technical plans
- Data Management outputs
- Configuration Management outputs
- Use existing plans as the foundation for the next life cycle phase.

## 5. Replanning Needs
Update technical planning based on:
- Technical Assessment results
- Technical Risk Management findings
- Decision Analysis outcomes

# Process Activities

## Technical Planning
- Define how the project will be technically organized, structured, and executed.
- Plan the application of the **17 NASA Systems Engineering Processes** across all product hierarchy levels and life cycle phases.
- Define the technical effort required to satisfy life cycle success criteria.

## Systems Engineering Management Plan (SEMP)
- Primary output of the Technical Planning Process.
- Subordinate to the Project Plan.
- Defines how the project will be technically managed.
- Describes the systems engineering management approach throughout the project lifecycle.

## Integration with Other Processes
Technical Planning is closely integrated with:
- Technical Risk Management
- Technical Assessment

This ensures corrective actions and project changes are incorporated into future technical activities.

## Technical Planning Focus
The technical team plans and manages:
- System decomposition
- System definition
- System integration
- Verification
- Validation
- Technical reviews
- Audits
- Assessments
- Status reporting
- Cross-cutting engineering activities
- Design verification requirements

# Technical Planning Preparation

Before execution:

- Define technical planning processes and procedures.
- Identify project participants.
- Assign roles and responsibilities.
- Determine required training and certifications.
- Select engineering tools, processes, and resources.
- Identify technical activities, expected work products, and schedules.
- Continuously refine plans as project information becomes available.

---

# Team Selection

Technical teams should include the appropriate mix of:

- Subsystem Engineers
- Discipline Engineers

Selection depends on:
- Product requirements
- Mission objectives
- Mission class
- Project life cycle phase
- Technical application
- Required engineering disciplines
- Availability of skilled personnel

The required skill mix evolves with mission complexity, technology requirements, and project phase.

## Technical Planning Strategy

A basic technical planning strategy should define:

### Communication
- Communication strategy within the technical team.
- Communication with project management, stakeholders, and external organizations.

### Requirements & Documentation
- Tailor applicable NASA procedural requirements.
- Define the required SEMP and supporting technical planning documentation.
- Identify and collect all required input documentation.

### Technical Work Planning
- Define the sequence of technical activities.
- Identify inputs and outputs for each activity.
- Define expected technical deliverables.
- Establish methods for capturing work products.

### Risk & Decision Management
- Identify and manage technical risks.
- Define the decision analysis approach.
- Plan contingency handling and replanning.

### Resources & Tools
- Identify required engineering tools, methods, and training.
- Estimate required effort and resource needs.
- Define technical metrics to measure and track progress.

### Stakeholder & Contractor Involvement
- Define stakeholder participation throughout the technical effort.
- Plan coordination with external contractors.

### Reviews & Interfaces
- Define entry and success criteria for:
  - Technical reviews
  - Project milestones
  - Life cycle phases
- Identify, define, and control internal and external interfaces.

### Knowledge & Technology Management
- Incorporate relevant lessons learned into planning.
- Capture new lessons learned during the project.
- Define the technology development approach and integration plan.

### Engineering Decisions
- Define criteria for:
  - Make, Buy, or Reuse decisions
  - COTS hardware and software selection
- Plan mitigation of off-nominal performance.

### Human Element
- Define the approach for managing the human element in technical activities and products.

---

## Planning Outcomes

A well-defined technical planning strategy provides:

- Clear understanding of the technical scope.
- Defined technical deliverables.
- Project schedule and key milestones.
- Required technical resources.
- Basis for estimating effort and planning future activities.

---

## Resource Estimation

- Estimate the effort required for each technical activity.
- Allocate appropriate personnel and resources.
- Avoid underestimating planning activities, as inadequate planning often leads to continuous project crisis management.

## Define the Technical Work

Define the technical effort appropriate for the current life cycle phase.

### Planning Considerations
- Estimate realistic cost, schedule, and labor resources.
- Use historical data or expert judgment for estimates.
- Include contingency based on project complexity and criticality.
- Continuously refine estimates as the project matures.

### Contingency Planning
Plan for unexpected technical issues such as:
- Additional software engineering support during hardware/system integration.
- Hardware-in-the-Loop (HWIL) debugging and testing.
- Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) evaluations to identify operational issues early.
- Allocate contingency resources to minimize cost and schedule impacts.

# 6.1.1.2.3 Schedule, Organize, and Budget the Technical Effort

## Scheduling

After defining the technical work:

- Organize tasks according to the Work Breakdown Structure (WBS).
- Establish task dependencies and execution sequence.
- Consider:
  - Project milestones
  - Funding availability
  - Resource availability
- Develop and maintain a network schedule using scheduling tools.

### Network Scheduling
A network schedule helps:
- Define activity dependencies.
- Identify the Critical Path.
- Calculate project duration.
- Determine activity float.
- Evaluate schedule impacts caused by technical or resource changes.

### Critical Path
- Longest sequence of dependent tasks.
- Determines overall project duration.
- Changes throughout the project and should be continuously monitored.

---

## Budgeting

Develop the project budget based on:
- Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)
- Network schedule
- Resource requirements

Budget elements include:
- Direct labor
- Overhead
- Other direct costs
- Subcontracts
- Materials
- Equipment
- General & administrative costs
- Cost of money (if applicable)
- Fee (if applicable)
- Contingency (UFE)

Budget, schedule, and WBS should remain closely integrated throughout the project.

# 6.1.1.2.4 Prepare the SEMP and Other Technical Plans

## Systems Engineering Management Plan (SEMP)

The SEMP is the primary technical management document for the project.

### Purpose
- Defines how the project will be technically managed.
- Documents the systems engineering approach.
- Describes technical processes, activities, organization, cost, and schedule.

### Characteristics
- Developed during the Formulation Phase.
- Tailored to each project.
- Living document that is continuously updated.
- Supports, but does not duplicate, other technical plans.

### Development
- Prepared by the technical team.
- Reviewed with the Project Manager.
- Requires contributions from technical and programmatic experts.
- Serves as the foundation for project resource planning.

---

## Role of the SEMP

The SEMP serves as the project's technical rulebook.

It:
- Defines technical management practices.
- Guides NASA and contractor engineering activities.
- Coordinates all technical plans.
- Supports project cost, schedule, risk, and resource planning.
- Should be updated whenever significant programmatic changes occur.

---

## Other Technical Plans

Depending on project complexity, additional plans may include:
- System Safety Plan
- Probabilistic Risk Assessment (PRA)
- Human Systems Integration (HSI) Plan
- Other project-specific technical plans

Standalone plans should be referenced within the SEMP.

---

## Verification Plan

Developed as part of Technical Planning and baselined at the Preliminary Design Review (PDR).

### Purpose
Defines:
- Verification methods
- Verification activities
- Verification cost
- Verification products

### Verification Methods
- Analysis
- Inspection
- Demonstration
- Test

Multiple verification methods may be required for a single requirement.

### Product Forms for Verification
Verification can be performed using different product maturity levels, including:
- Concept description
- Breadboard
- Brassboard
- Engineering Unit (EMU)
- Prototype
- Qualification Unit
- Protoflight Unit
- Flight Unit
- Simulation
- Mock-up
